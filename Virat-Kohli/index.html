Virat Kohli

Early Life

According to en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virat_Kohli, Virat Kohli was born on 5, November, 1988 in a punjabi family. His father, Prem kohli worked as a criminal lawyer and his mother, Saroj Kohli is a housewife. He has an older brother, Vikas and an older sister, Bhavna. According to his family, when he was three years old, Kohli would pick up a cricket bat, start swinging it and ask his father to bowl at him. Kohli was raised in Uttam Nagar and started his schooling at Vishal Bharti Public School. In 1988, the West Delhi Cricket Academy was created, and Kohli, a nine-year-old, was part of its first intake. Kohli's fatehr took him to the academy after their neighbours suggested that "Virat shouldn't waste his time in street cricket and instead join a proffesional club". Kohli trained at the academy under Rajkumar Sharma. Sharma recounts Kohli's early days at his academy, "He oozed talent. It was so difficult to keep him quiet. He was natural in whatever he did and I was most impressed with his attitude. He was ready to bat at any spot and I had to litteraly push him home after the training sessions. He just wouldn't leave. Apart from sports, Kohli was good at academics as well, and his teachers remember him as a "bright and alert child".

Youth and Domestic Career

Kohli first played for Delhi Under-15 team in October 2002 in the 2002-03 Polly Umrigar Trophy. He was the leading run getter for his team in that tournament with 172 runs at an average of 34.40. He became the captain of the team for the 2003-04 Polly Umrigar Trophy and scored 390 runs in 5 innings at average of 78 including two centuries and two fifties. In late 2004, he was selected in the Delhi Under-17 for the 2003-04 Vijay Merchant Trophy. He scored 470 runs in four matches at an average of 117.50 with two hundreds and top score of 251*. In July, 2006, Kohli was selected in India Under 19 squad on its tour of England. He averaged 105 in three-match ODI seires against England Under 19s and 49 in three match test series. Kohli made his first class debut for Delhi against Tamil Nadu in November 2006, at the age of 18, and scored 10 in his debut innings. He came into the spotlight in December when he decided to play for his team against Karnatka on the day after his father's death and went on to score 90. He went directly to funeral after he was dismissed.His mother noted that "Virat changed a bit after that day. Overnight he became a much more matured person". In April 2007, he made his Twenty20 debut and finished as the highest run getter for his team in the Inter-State T20 Championship. In February-March 2008, Kohli captained the victorious Indian team at the 2008 ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup held in Malyasia. Batting at number 3, he scored a235 runs in 6 matches at an average of 47 and he was one of the three batsman to score a hundred in the tournament. Following the Under-19 World Cup , Kohli was bought by the Indian Preimere League franchise Royal Challengers Banglore for $30,000 on a youth contract.

International Career

In August 2008, Kohli was included in the Indian ODI squad for tour of Sri-Lanka and the Champions Trophy in Pakistan. Prior to the Sri Lankan tour, Kohli had played only eight List A matches, his selection was called a "surprise call-up". At the age of 19 he made his interenational debut and was dismissed for 12. He made his first half-century, a score of 54, in the fourth match which helped India win the series. In October 2008, Kohli played for Indian Board Presidents XI in a four day tour match against Australia. He made 105 and 16* in that match against a bowling line up consisting of Brett Lee, Stuart Clarck, Mitchell Johnson, Peter Sidle and Jason Krejza. He made his T20 debut against Zimbabwe at Harare and scored unbeaten 26. He was Leading run scorer in ODIs in 2010 with 995 runs from 25 matches at an average of 47.38 including three centuries He jumped to number two spot on the ICC Rankings for ODI batsmen in year 2011. Kohli played every match of the India's successful World Cup campaign. He scored an unbeaten 100, his fifth ODI century on World Cup debut. Kohli stood in as the captain for the first Odi of the trianhular series in West Indies after Dhoni injured himself. In his second match as captain he scored his first hundered as captain making 102 off 83 balls against West Indies at Port of Spain in a bonus point win. Kohli had a succesful run with the bat in the seven match ODI series against Australia. As of August 2019, Kohli has scored 25 test centuries and 43 ODI centuries which makes him second best centurian in ODI after Sachin Tendulkar.
